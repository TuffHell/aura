What it takes to build a body that can hold a patient.

The experience shows what AURA does. This page is the engineer's answer to "could we actually build it?" — the physical requirements, force budgets, sensing stack, care effectors, power system, and the safety case, with the honest line drawn around what a home care robot must never be allowed to do by itself.

01

Morphology — a soft machine on a stiff spine

Everything a patient touches is inflated and compliant; everything that carries load is a slender internal skeleton the skin can never pinch against.

  1. 1
    Head sensor suite. 60 GHz mmWave radar (heart & breath, through blankets), radiometric thermal camera with an internal black-body reference (±0.2 °C), RGB-D, and a 4-mic array behind acoustically transparent skin.
  2. 2
    Chest access port & heartlight. Service hatch for the pressure regulator and filters; the ring-light doubles as the state indicator patients actually read (teal calm → amber escalation).
  3. 3
    Series-elastic arms. Torque is delivered through a measured spring, so the arm *is* its own force sensor — back-drivable at any moment, mechanically incapable of exceeding the hug budget.
  4. 4
    Dose & compress bay (forearm). Pharmacy-sealed unit-dose carousel with chain-of-custody logging, and a Peltier-chilled gel compress at 18–21 °C.
  5. 5
    Base: battery & dock. LiFePO₄ pack low in the pelvis for an un-tippable centre of mass; charge contacts in the footprint for the night dock.
  6. 6
    Stiff spine, soft everything else. A carbon-fibre/7075 column carries all loads. The skin is an inflated bladder at ~1.3 kPa — a firm cushion, never a shell.

02

Touch — the hug as a controlled dose

Deep-pressure therapy is a dosed mechanical intervention. The budget below is the whole point of the machine: strong enough to feel held, weak enough to be inherently safe.

QuantityRequirementWhy
Embrace pressure2.7–5.3 kPa (20–40 mmHg) distributed over ≥ 600 cm² The clinically studied deep-pressure band — clearly below tissue-injury thresholds.
Peak contact force≤ 65 N per arm, quasi-static Inside ISO/TS 15066 body-region limits for the thorax with a compliant surface.
ReleaseBack-drivable in ≤ 150 ms; passive release on power loss Springs open the arms when de-energised — a patient can always simply stand up.
Pinch pointsNone accessible < 25 mmGaps are bridged by the inflated skin itself.
Skin warmth36–40 °C zones, hardware-limited at 41 °C Contact-heating limits per IEC 60601 thinking: warm presence, zero burn risk even asleep against it.
Squeeze rhythmExhale-locked, ≤ 0.15 Hz modulation Pressure pulses ride the patient's own breath — co-regulation, not restraint.

The governing principle: safe by construction, not by software.

No line of code is trusted to keep the hug gentle. The actuators cannot produce an unsafe force: series-elastic elements saturate mechanically, the skin distributes load, and power loss opens the arms. Software only ever makes the machine gentler than its hardware ceiling.

04

Care effectors — what the hands actually do

Each capability in the experience's care phase is a physical module with a deliberately narrow mandate.

Dose carousel

14 × sealed unit doses

Loaded and sealed by the pharmacy, not the robot. AURA presents the scheduled blister, verifies intake on camera, and logs to the medication record. It cannot open, split, or substitute a dose — adherence support, not prescribing.

Thermal comfort

18–21 °Ccompress

Peltier-chilled gel pad, force-limited placement against the forehead, auto-lift on any head movement. Comfort measure only; the thermal camera keeps reading the fever underneath.

Hydration

150 mlcup dock

Presents water with a compliant two-finger grip (≤ 8 N). Intake is logged for the deterioration model — dehydration is a sepsis-relapse accelerant.

Emergency loop

< 30 sto a human

Red-flag speech or vitals open a nurse video/audio channel on the chest surface and, if unanswered, escalate to emergency services with location and the vitals packet.

05

Procedure tiers — and the line we will not cross

"Can it do mini surgeries?" is the right question to ask and the wrong thing to build. Here is the full capability ladder, tier by tier, with the honest engineering and regulatory reasons for where it stops.

TierCapabilityAutonomyStatus
T0 · ComfortDeep-pressure hold, warmth, breath pacing, cooling compress AutonomousIn the experience
T1 · Monitoring & adherenceContactless vitals, scheduled-dose dispensing, hydration, sleep watch Autonomous, guardrailedIn the experience
T2 · Guided first aidCoaches the human: wound-dressing guidance, CPR pacing on its own chest, AED retrieval, choking protocol Robot guides, human actsRoadmap
T3 · Tele-examinationClinician drives: auscultation head, otoscope/dermatoscope presentation, guided palpation with force echo Clinician-in-the-loopRoadmap
TX · Invasive proceduresInjections, venipuncture, suturing, incision — any breach of skin —Excluded by design

Why AURA will never do surgery — a position, not a shrug.

1 · Physics. Every safety property above comes from compliance — low pressure, soft skin, saturating actuators. Invasive work needs the opposite: rigid, sub-millimetre, high-stiffness manipulation. One body cannot be maximally soft and surgically stiff; bolting a scalpel to a hug machine forfeits the safety case that lets it live unsupervised in a bedroom.
2 · Regulation. T0–T1 track as a Class II device with a software-as-medical-device pathway. Skin-breaching autonomy is Class III territory — surgical robots ship with a credentialed surgeon at a console, in an OR, with sterile field and anaesthesia support. None of that exists at 2 a.m. in Elena's bedroom.
3 · The mission. The clinical gap AURA fills is the lonely deterioration window between discharge and readmission. What closes it is monitoring, adherence, co-regulation, and fast escalation to humans — precisely T0–T3. When something needs cutting, AURA's job is to have already called the people with the scalpel.

06

Power, compute, quiet

A night-care robot earns its place by being trustworthy at 3 a.m.: silent, cool, never dead when reached for.

Battery

810 WhLiFePO₄, 25.6 V

Chemistry chosen for thermal stability in a device people sleep against. ≥ 14 h active duty, 3,000+ cycles, cell-level fusing, charges only on the dock.

Compute

25 Wtypical · 60 W peak

Jetson-class module. All perception, speech, and the deterioration model run on-device; the conversational brain may be cloud (as in the talk prototype) but safety logic never is.

Acoustics

< 35 dB(A)night mode, 1 m

Blower on fluid bearings, oversized slow fans, bladder acts as its own muffler. Quieter than the fridge two rooms away.

Thermals

≤ 43 °Cany touchable point

Skin is the radiator: the bladder's circulating air carries compute heat to the full body surface — which is also what makes the body feel warm-blooded.

The safety & compliance stack

The paperwork is part of the machine. Target conformance, in the order an auditor would ask for it:

StandardScopeAURA obligation
ISO 13482Personal care robotsThe core safety envelope: contact forces, stability, emergency stop, autonomous-behaviour limits.
ISO/TS 15066Collaborative force limitsBody-region pressure/force ceilings adopted for every contact behaviour, hug included.
IEC 60601-1 / -1-2Medical electrical safety / EMCPatient-contact electrical safety; radar/radio coexistence with home medical devices.
IEC 62304 + ISO 14971Software lifecycle + risk managementThe deterioration model and escalation logic developed as Class B/C software with a living risk file.
IEC 81001-5-1Health-software cybersecuritySigned firmware, no remote actuation path, radio-silent "sanctuary mode" that keeps all T0 functions local.
HIPAA / GDPRPatient dataVitals processed on-device; only FHIR escalation packets leave, over TLS, to the named care team. No cloud audio or video at rest.

Failure posture: every fault degrades toward softer and more human — sensors fail → escalate to a person; power fails → arms open; network fails → local care continues, sirenless, while the dock's landline path carries the red-flag call. The one thing AURA is never allowed to fail at is handing you to a human.
